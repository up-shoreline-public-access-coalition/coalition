The UP Parks, Recreation, and Open Space (PROS) plan gets updated every 6 years, defining a vision for development in our public spaces. As far as we understand, having this document is a necessity when applying for grants to aid in funding such development. Just because something is included in the PROS plan doesn't mean it is going to happen, but for anything to happen it has to be in the PROS plan. This document can also be amended outside of the regular update cycle if the city council identifies the need, but that is infrequent.

2020 PROS Plan (starts on page 130 of PDF):
https://wa-universityplace.civicplus.com/AgendaCenter/ViewFile/Item/23?fileID=122

## Analysis Summary

The 2020 UP PROS plan acknowledges that access to the Puget Sound shoreline is limited in UP, and declares goals that would address this. During the public outreach conducted, shoreline access was a common priority among the community. Despite all this, the plan itself doesn't include *any* shoreline sites. We couldn't find any explanation in the document to explain why improving or expanding shoreline access was not planned for.
