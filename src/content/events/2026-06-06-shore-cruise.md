**Update: This event was postponed from June 6th to June 13th due to bad weather.**

**Update: We shortened the route to make it more casual. This page has been updated accordingly.**

> ***RSVP by email (or ask any questions): [up-shoreline-public-access@protonmail.com](mailto:up-shoreline-public-access@protonmail.com)***
> Let us know your name(s), what non-motorized watercraft you plan to use, and anything else we should know to support you!

Come join our first ever UP shore cruise! Bring your own non-motorized watercraft and see the northern University Place shoreline from the water! Please set aside ~4 hours for the event.

No experience necessary. Staying near the shore with a life jacket is relatively safe, but join at your own risk ([see our safety guidance](/safety)). Entire ride is 3.7 miles round trip, so having a comfortable and efficient setup will make the trip easier. 

Time and date are subject to change based on weather, so RSVP and we will keep you updated! 

**Agenda:**

1. Set off from Day Island Public Beach (1898 Day Island Blvd W, University Place, WA 98466).
    - Allow time to find parking (treat like normal neighborhood parking). No bike racks or lockers available.
    - No public amenities are available on Day Island or anywhere along the route. Bring your own water bottle and use the bathroom before coming!
1. Cruise south, hugging the shoreline.
    - Be stewards of the hard-to-access shoreline along the way:
        - Collect any garbage we might spot.
        - Report any distressed marine mammals we might see.
        - Support any distressed humans we might see.
        - Monitor for any concerning shoreline development.
    - Stop to acknowledge public access points along the way.
1. Stop at Sunset Beach public access.
1. Turn around and cruise back north, hugging the shoreline.
1. Arrive back at Day Island Public Beach.
1. (Tentative) Pop into Day Island Lagoon
    - This is the northernmost shoreline, but we might omit depending on time and energy.

**What to bring?**

1. Life jacket
1. Sun protection (lotion, hat, glasses, etc.)
1. Non-motorized watercraft (w/ paddles)
1. Water bottle
1. Snack(s) that won't make a mess

#### Update

On 2026-06-05, we decided to postpone the event because the weather forecast shows lightning, rain, and lower air temperatures. We think people will be safer and more comfortable this way. 

On 2026-05-23 we did a trial run of the originally proposed route (out and back from Day Island to Chambers Creek Regional Park) and found that, while doable within the 4 hour window, it would require moving at a non-casual pace. In the spirit of keeping this event casual and beginner friendly, we have opted to shorten the route by turning around at Sunset Beach instead. This reduces the distance we will need to travel by 2.5 miles. We will try to organize events in the future to cover the portions of shoreline that we won't see in this event.

Unfortunately, this now means that there are no public restrooms, drinking fountains, or trash cans anywhere along our route.
